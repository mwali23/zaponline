# Launch and growth plan

## Phase 1 — trusted utility (6–8 weeks)

Launch district status, accounts, saved-area alerts and report confirmations in two Copperbelt cities. Recruit community ambassadors and establish an escalation channel with utilities/local authorities. Measure report confirmation rate, time-to-first-confirmation and alert usefulness—not raw signups.

## Phase 2 — assisted marketplace (8–12 weeks)

Onboard 15–25 providers manually across solar, electrical safety and generator repair. ZAP staff review every profile and help customers create structured requests. Start with quote/documentation workflows; enable money movement only after contracting a licensed local processor and defining disputes, refunds and merchant-of-record responsibilities.

## Phase 3 — impact partnerships

Pilot a small number of NGO/community projects with clear ownership and maintenance plans. Sponsors fund defined milestones; ZAP publishes evidence and outcome updates. Avoid presenting donations as charitable deductions unless the receiving entity is legally eligible.

## Growth loops

- An outage alert links to a one-tap confirmation, improving the map for the next user.
- A useful report prompts the user to save an area, improving retention.
- Completed jobs create verified reviews, improving marketplace conversion.
- Providers share their public ZAP profile, bringing customers at low acquisition cost.
- Impact updates are shareable evidence for NGO and corporate partners.

## Channels

- WhatsApp community groups and opt-in alert sharing.
- Local radio power-status segments with a consistent ZAP URL/short code.
- Clinics, schools, markets and business associations as trusted distribution partners.
- Provider referral kits and QR-coded verification profiles.
- SEO landing pages for district status and high-intent services.

## North-star and guardrail metrics

- North star: weekly users who receive a useful status update or complete a trusted service interaction.
- Intelligence: confirmed-report rate, median age of active status, false-report/moderation rate.
- Marketplace: request-to-qualified-proposal rate, completion rate, repeat customer rate, disputes.
- Impact: milestone timeliness, evidence completeness, operational uptime after installation.
- Guardrails: safety incidents, privacy complaints, provider concentration and paid-acquisition payback.
